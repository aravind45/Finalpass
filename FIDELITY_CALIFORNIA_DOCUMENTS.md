# Fidelity Estate Settlement Documents - California

## Required Documents for Fidelity (California Estates)

### üìã Core Documents (Always Required)

#### 1. **Certified Death Certificate**
- **Format:** Certified copy (original not required)
- **Quantity:** 1 copy for Fidelity
- **Where to get:** California Department of Public Health or County Vital Records
- **Cost:** ~$25 per certified copy
- **Timeline:** 1-2 weeks to receive
- **Important:** Must be a "certified copy" with raised seal, not a photocopy

#### 2. **Letters Testamentary (or Letters of Administration)**
- **Format:** Certified copy acceptable (original not required for Fidelity)
- **Quantity:** 1 copy
- **Where to get:** California Probate Court after probate filing
- **Timeline:** 4-8 weeks after filing probate petition
- **Important:** Must be current (not expired) and show your authority as executor

#### 3. **Fidelity Estate Settlement Packet**
- **What it is:** Fidelity-specific forms for account transfer/closure
- **How to get:** 
  - Call Fidelity Estate Services: 800-544-0003
  - Request "Estate Settlement Packet"
  - Or download from Fidelity.com (if available)
- **Forms typically included:**
  - Account Transfer Request
  - Beneficiary Designation Verification
  - Distribution Authorization
  - W-9 for Estate Tax ID

---

## üìù Additional Documents (Often Required)

#### 4. **Estate Tax ID Number (EIN)**
- **What it is:** IRS-issued identification number for the estate
- **When needed:** If estate earns income or has multiple beneficiaries
- **How to get:** Apply online at IRS.gov (Form SS-4)
- **Timeline:** Immediate (online) or 2-4 weeks (by mail)
- **Cost:** Free

#### 5. **Medallion Signature Guarantee**
- **What it is:** Bank certification of your signature (like notarization but stricter)
- **When needed:** For transfers over $100,000 or certain account types
- **Where to get:** 
  - Your bank (if you're a customer)
  - Credit union
  - Brokerage firm
- **Cost:** Usually free for customers
- **Important:** NOT the same as notarization - must be Medallion Signature Guarantee

#### 6. **Account Statements**
- **What it is:** Most recent statements for the deceased's accounts
- **When needed:** Helpful but not always required
- **Where to get:** 
  - Deceased's files
  - Request from Fidelity (may require death certificate first)

#### 7. **Beneficiary Information**
- **What it is:** Names, addresses, SSNs of all beneficiaries
- **When needed:** For distribution of assets
- **Format:** Fidelity will provide forms

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Issues & Solutions

### Issue 1: "We need the original death certificate"
**Solution:** Fidelity accepts certified copies. If they insist, escalate to supervisor. California law allows certified copies.

### Issue 2: "Your Letters Testamentary are expired"
**Solution:** Letters don't expire in California, but Fidelity may want recent ones. Request updated certified copies from court ($15 each).

### Issue 3: "We need a Medallion Signature Guarantee"
**Solution:** 
- Go to your bank (must be a customer)
- Bring: Letters Testamentary, government ID, Fidelity forms
- Ask specifically for "Medallion Signature Guarantee" (not notarization)

### Issue 4: "We can't find any accounts"
**Solution:**
- Check tax returns for 1099 forms
- Search mail for statements
- Call Fidelity with SSN and date of birth (after providing death certificate)

### Issue 5: "We need beneficiary designation forms"
**Solution:** These should be in the Estate Settlement Packet. If not, request them specifically.

## üí° Pro Tips

### 1. **Order Extra Death Certificates**
Get 10-15 certified copies initially. You'll need them for:
- Fidelity
- Other financial institutions
- Social Security
- Insurance companies
- DMV (vehicle titles)
- Real estate transfers

### 2. **Keep Copies of Everything**
- Scan all documents before sending
- Use certified mail with tracking
- Keep a log of what you sent and when

### 3. **Don't Wait for Probate to Start Research**
You can:
- Identify accounts
- Request estate packets
- Prepare forms
- Just can't submit until you have Letters Testamentary

### 4. **Ask About Beneficiary Designations**
If accounts have named beneficiaries, they may bypass probate entirely. Ask Fidelity to check.

### 5. **Request Direct Deposit**
For distributions, provide bank account info for direct deposit (faster than checks).

## üö® When to Escalate

Escalate to Fidelity supervisor if:
- No response after 14 days
- Conflicting information from different agents
- Unreasonable document requests
- Processing delays beyond 8 weeks
- Account access issues

**How to escalate:**
1. Call main number: 800-544-0003
2. Ask for "Estate Services Supervisor"
3. Reference your case number
4. Explain the issue calmly
5. Request timeline for resolution
6. Get supervisor's direct contact info
